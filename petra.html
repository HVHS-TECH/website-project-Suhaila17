Petra: The Rose City

Petra is an ancient city carved into the rose-colored rock formations of southern Jordan. Known as the Rose City, Petra was the capital of the Nabataean Kingdom in the 4th century BC. The city is renowned for its unique rock-cut architecture and water conduit system. Its most famous structure is the Al-Khazneh (Treasury), a grand facade that stands at the entrance to the Siq, a narrow gorge.

Petra’s Beginnings

The origins of Petra date back to at least the 4th century BC, but it reached its height in the 1st century AD under the Nabataeans. The city’s impressive rock-cut architecture was not only a feat of engineering but also served as a strategic location for controlling trade routes between Arabia, Egypt, and the Mediterranean.
